Ceratina hieroglyphica

Hymenoptera

Small carpenter bees (Ceratina) are typically dark, shiny, often metallic-looking bees with sparse body hair. They are small in size and commonly have yellow markings, especially on the face, which often leads to them being mistaken for sweat bees.

Canthophorus niveimarginatus

Hemiptera

True bugs (Hemiptera) are characterized by piercing-sucking mouthparts formed into a beak-like "rostrum," and forewings that are often partially hardened at the base and membranous at the tips—a feature that gives the order its name, "half-winged."

Pselliophora bifascipennis

Diptera

Crane flies in the family Tipulidae are characterized by their long maxillary palps (appendages near the mouth), where the fourth segment is longer than the first three combined. They also typically have 13 segments in their antennae.

Clogmia albipunctata

Diptera

The white-dotted drain fly (Clogmia albipunctata) is a very small (about 2–3 mm), moth-like insect with broad, hairy wings marked with distinct black and white spots, and a body covered in fine grayish hairs.

Yemma exilis

Hemiptera

Stilt bugs (Berytidae) are extremely slender insects with remarkably long, thin legs and antennae that often have a distinctive club-like swelling at the tip. Their bodies are typically brown to yellow, and some species have slight swellings at the ends of their leg segments.

Tetramorium caespitum

Tetramorium caespitum are typically small, reddish-brown ants commonly recognized by the grooves or lines on their head and thorax, and a stinger at the tip of their abdomen.

Nylanderia flavipes (Yellow-footed Ant)

Nylanderia ants are small to medium-sized (1–4 mm) and range in color from pale yellow to black. Workers are generally uniform in appearance with a compact, robust body, six mandibular teeth, and characteristic erect hairs on their legs, antennae, and specific body segments.

Scaled Hump-backed Fly (Megaselia scalar)

Megaselia scalaris is a very small (about 2 mm) yellowish fly with dark markings, a distinctive humpbacked profile, and translucent wings. Its mouthparts feature sharp teeth, and males have specialized structures at the tip of the abdomen.

Piratula piratoides

Wolf spiders are robust, agile hunters with a body size ranging from less than 10 mm to 35 mm. They are easily identified by their unique eye arrangement: three rows of eyes, with the middle row containing two very large eyes that give them excellent vision.

Hydrotaea flies are small, typically 5.75–8.5 mm in length, with colors ranging from light brown to bluish-black. They have large red eyes, plumose antennae, and their larvae are carnivorous, often featuring ventral welts and large spiracles.

Pyralid moths, often called snout moths, are generally small to medium-sized with wingspans ranging from 9 to 37 mm. They are named for their prominent mouthparts (labial palps) that often extend forward like a snout.

The Asian needle ant (Brachyponera chinensis) is a ponerine ant, likely characterized by a typical ant body plan with a distinct head, thorax, and abdomen, and possessing a sting.

12-Spot Ladybird

Vibidia duodecimguttata, the twelve-spotted ladybird, is a small, oval beetle about 4 mm long. It has a light brown body and distinctive white spots—twelve in total, with six on each wing case (elytron).

Prodiamesa olivacea

Chironomids, or non-biting midges, are small flies that resemble mosquitoes but lack scales on their wings and biting mouthparts. The males are easily recognized by their feathery (plumose) antennae. Some larvae are bright red, known as "bloodworms," due to a hemoglobin-like substance..

Twenty-eight Spotted Lady Beetle

Coleoptera

The 28-spotted potato ladybird has a nearly round, glossy, reddish-brown body up to 7 mm long, with 13 black spots on each wing cover and additional spots on the thorax. Its larvae and pupae are yellow-green with black, branched spines.

Lasius niger

Hymenoptera

Black garden ants, Lasius niger, are small, glossy black insects where workers measure 3–5 mm, queens are larger (up to 9 mm) and lose their wings after mating, and males are slender, wasp-like, and slightly smaller than queens.

Asian Tiger Mosquito (Aedes albopictus)

Diptera

The Asian tiger mosquito (Aedes albopictus) is a small mosquito, less than 10 mm in length, with a distinctive black-and-white pattern. It has white bands on its legs and body, a single silvery-white line running down the thorax, and white-scaled spots on its abdomen and legs.

Rush Veneer

Lepidoptera

The Rush Veneer moth (Nomophila noctuella) has a wingspan of 26–32 mm, with narrow, elongated brown forewings featuring distinctive darker markings, and whitish hindwings with brown veins. The mature larvae are gray-green and can grow up to 15–20 mm in length.

Hymenoptera

Paraparatrechina ants are very small, typically only 1-2 mm long. They often have a distinctive metallic iridescence, showing blue, purple, or pink hues under light, and possess a unique pattern of erect hairs on their bodies.
